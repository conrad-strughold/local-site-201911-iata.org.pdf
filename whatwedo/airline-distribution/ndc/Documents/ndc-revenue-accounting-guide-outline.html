  • Background
  • Stages in NDC Offer and Order Management
    • 1. Online itineraries
      • 1.1 NDC airline publishes its ‘Airline Profile’
      • 1.2 Agent accreditation and security for cash settlement when an agent confirms payment
      • 1.3 Agent initiates Shopping Request and receives Offer(s) in response from Offer Responsible Airlines (ORAs)
      • 1.4 Agent places an Order and receives confirmation of Order and Reservation
      • 1.5 Agent provides proposed form of payment, ORA issues accountable documents and returns itinerary receipt
      • 1.6 ORA feeds sales data from their Ticketing and Order Management System (OMS) into their Revenue Accounting Systems (RAS)
      • 1.7 Validating Carrier chooses to report sale to BSP, triggering agent billing and HOT file production
      • 1.8 BSP conducts remittance from agent and settles with ORA
      • 1.9 Operating Carriers - DCS and airport coupon control processes
      • 1.10 Ticketing Servers report final status coupons to RA Systems
      • 1.11 Revenue Accounting Systems conduct accounting, prepare reports
    • 2. Use of NDC for managing Interline itineraries
      • 2.1 Agent initiates Shopping Request with an Offer Responsible Airlines (ORA). ORA needs to acquire content from partner airlines to satisfy the request.
      • 2.2 Agent request Order creation and receives confirmation. ORA will advise any POA of the acceptance of their interline Offers.
      • 2.3 Agent provides proposed form of payment, ORA issues documents, and advises any interline partners
      • 2.4 All airlines feed sales data into their Revenue Accounting systems from their Ticketing and OMSs
      • 2.5 Revenue Accounting Systems conduct accounting, prepare reports and manage interline billing through SIS
    • 3. Use of NDC for managing Interline itineraries with codeshare
