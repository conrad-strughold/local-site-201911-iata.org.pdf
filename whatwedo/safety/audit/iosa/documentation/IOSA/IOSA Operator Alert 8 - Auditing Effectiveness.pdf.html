Auditing Effectiveness of Implementation 

Issue date: 4-JAN-2018 

Effective date: 4-JAN-2018

Introduction 

 

IOSA requires a two-stage auditing function, the assessment of the provision being 
documented, followed by an assessment of the provision being implemented. This 
core IOSA principle is to ensure that the implementation assessment is based on 
standard operating practices and not on undocumented operating practices, for 
which standardization cannot be assured.  

To improve the quality and completeness of the IOSA evaluations, the element of 
effectiveness will be introduced into the assessment of the ISARP implementation. 
The purpose of the assessment is to validate if an ISARP has been implemented 
effectively or not, in accordance with criteria established in the IOSA Standards 
Manual (ISM). 

Conformity with an ISARP will still be based on documentation and implementation 
and the method to assess these will not change. No new requirements will be 
introduced for an operator to obtain or to renew an IOSA registration. The 
assessment of effectiveness will be performed in addition to the existing auditing and 
will initially be limited to only a small number of ISARPs.  

It is important to stress at this point that an effective implementation should be 
achieved by the operators for all ISARPs, not only the ones covered by this method. 
For practical purposes, the formal assessment by the auditors is initially limited to the 
specified ISARPs. 

Standards Assessment 

Auditing effective implementation will initially be applied only to a selected number of 
ISARPs. The overall result assessing these ISARPs for effective implementation will be 
captured in a single recommended practice, which will be introduced into the ISM. 
Hence, this is not a standard and therefore will not be mandatory. The conformity of 
the assessed ISARPs is not dependant on the auditor’s assessment of effectiveness. 

The methodology will initially be applied to the following ISARPs: 



 

ORG 3.1.2 – Risk Assessment and Mitigation and all its repeated provisions 



 

ORG 3.3.13 – Flight Data Analysis

The assessment of effectiveness will be based on the two aspects of suitability and 
specific effectiveness criteria. Suitability will be reviewed using general criteria, 
whereas the effectiveness criteria will be specifically listed and individually assessed. 

The recommended practice, as well as the suitability and effectiveness criteria will be 
released through a Temporary Revision to the ISM in early 2019. 

To ensure an objective and consistent assessment of effectiveness, IATA, the IOSA 
Oversight Council, and the relevant IOSA Technical Groups are in the process of 
developing detailed guidance and procedures for the Audit Organizations, IOSA 
auditors, as well as the operators. This guidance will be made available in due time to 
ensure all parties have sufficient time to study and familiarize themselves with it. The 
auditors will also be trained specifically in this new method.  

Implementation Plan 

It is planned to implement the methodology for audits taking place in the second 
quarter of 2019. The first three months will serve as a proof-of-concept period with 
close monitoring of the method for potential adjustments, to be completed with the 
effective date of ISM Edition 13 in September 2019. 

IATA will distribute all guidance material well in advance of the beginning of the proof 
of concept phase and the implementation of the methodology.
