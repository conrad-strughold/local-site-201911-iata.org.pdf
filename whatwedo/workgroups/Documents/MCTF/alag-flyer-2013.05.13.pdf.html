What is ALAG?

IATA’s Aircraft Leasing Advisory Group (ALAG) was 

created to assist in all matters relating to aircraft leasing, 

make recommendations related thereto, and indicate how 

its recommendations may be implemented. Additionally, 

ALAG provides a neutral forum for airlines, leasing 

companies and others to come together and address 

industry issues related to the aircraft leasing process.

What does ALAG do?

The ALAG aims at developing cost-effective policies, 

standards, and recommendations relating to:
•  Paperless documentation to support aircraft transfers
•  Aircraft leasing lifecycle
•  Delivery/redelivery conditions
•  Methodology on maintenance reserves
•  Aircraft parts replacement
•  Back to birth record 
•  Co-operation and co-ordination of activities with 

other relevant entities, airline regional associations 

and other international bodies as necessary to 

achieve industry goals and objectives.

Why Join ALAG?

ALAG provides a forum for airlines, and other industry 

stakeholders (such as lessors, and service providers) 

to exchange and share knowledge, best practices and 

industry know-how, as well as networking in the aircraft 

leasing area. 

How to Join ALAG?

Membership to the ALAG is open to Member Airlines, 

IATA Strategic Partners and any other specialists who 

are approved by the ALAG Steering Committee.
