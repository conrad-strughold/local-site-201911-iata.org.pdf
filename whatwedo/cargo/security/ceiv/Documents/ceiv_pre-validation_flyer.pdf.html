What happens on 1

st

 July 2014

 

All affected air cargo and mail carriers operating into the EU and EEA from third countries (ACC3) 

must obtain an independent validation of their security program to maintain their ACC3 designation.

 

All affected carriers (ACC3) will only be allowed to accept screened/secured air cargo or mail from 

entities that have been subject to EU accredited independent validation.

 

All affected carriers (ACC3) will be accountable for screening and securing of cargo flown into the EU 

or EEA at the last point of departure (LPD) and any upstream locations for transfer and transit cargo.

 

Regulations impact the supply chains of all carriers (ACC3) in the form of regulated agents, known 

consignors, and entities that screen and secure cargo on behalf of the carriers.

What we provide

To help you prepare for your independent validation, IATA CEIV now provides in-house readiness 

workshops followed by pre-validation assessments conducted by an IATA CEIV trained and EU 

accredited Independent Validator (IV).

Our basic service package includes: 

 

Off-site review of your security program (examples: training manuals, equipment handbook and 

processes)

 

In-company hub location readiness training workshop for up to 20 of your employees

 

On-site assessment of your hub location

Following the assessment, you will receive a pre-validation report with the findings and recommended 

best practice suggestions to better enable you to take appropriate actions prior to your independent 

validation.
