Issues and expectations

Ê

Thousands carrier stations will have to be audited by July 
2014+ (phased in approach) and re-audited (5 years)

Ê

Airlines are concerned  with significant costs and meeting 
deadlines and are asking IATA for a solution

Ê

Other parties (direct shippers, forwarders and handlers) will 
have to be validated if they wish to apply security 
measures

Ê

Some EU Member States are also concerned about 
meeting deadlines and want industry support (e.g. IATA)

Benefits

Regulators, Industry and IATA would benefit from the IATA 
Center of Excellence for Independent Validators:

Ê

Regulators would have a reliable approach to have 
independent validators auditing airlines and other parties

Ê

Industry would have less costs as validations will be 
“mutualized” and potentially part of other audit programs 

Ê

IATA would serve its members helping them to meet 
new cargo security regulations

Ê

The program could be expanded to validate handlers, 
forwarders and direct shippers

Ê

The program could meet the core requirements of other 
Regulators
