Airport Charges at Queen Alia International Airport:  letter from the Director 
General of IATA to the Prime Minister and Minister of Defense of the 
Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, H.E. Mr Nader A. Dahabi 

 
 
 

Geneva, 21 October 2009 

 
 
Your Excellency, 
 
 
For many years, Jordan has been regarded as a leader in aviation, with the support of its 
enlightened leadership and Your Excellency’s personal commitment towards aviation 
industry. 
 
I am writing to seek your urgent support and to draw your attention to our deepest 
concern regarding some serious shortcomings with the Rehabilitation, Expansion & 
Operation Agreement (REOA) for Queen Alia International Airport (QAIA) between the 
Government and the Airline International Group (AIG).  
 
Our understanding is that this agreement allows for retroactive application of inflation-
linked charges increases, does not require consultation with the airlines on changes to 
charges levels and allows an excessive 54% concession fee on turnover to the 
Government. In our view this is counter-productive to the economic and social 
development of Jordan and the benefits that civil aviation can provide. 
  
As we have said to AIG, at this time of global economic crisis, we do not believe that 
inflation-linked charges increases are appropriate. Airport charges should be closely 
related to the actual facilities and services provided and subject to meaningful 
consultation with airlines in line with ICAO principles. Retroactive charges are similarly 
inappropriate in that they are paid by future passengers and airlines rather than those 
who have used the facilities.  
 
In our experience, the 54% concession fee is unacceptably excessive. Given that the 
Government has largely withdrawn from ownership and investment in QAIA, the 
concession fee can be regarded as an additional cost to the airlines and passengers. 
This results in higher ticket prices that affect demand, possible damage to the aviation 
industry and an adverse impact on economic growth and tourism. Growth in air traffic, 
tourism and economic aviation is related to lower aviation costs which guarantee 
additional jobs and revenue for Government, that more than offsets direct revenue from 
the concession fee. These benefits can better be realised through the elimination or 
reduction of the concession fee.

On 12 October a joint IATA/AACO letter was sent to the Minister of Transport, outlining 
the charges and taxes issues in Jordan in general and highlighting the REOA problems 
in particular.  This letter called for a meeting to address these issues. I would be most 
grateful for your support in arranging this meeting to produce positive results that show 
Jordan’s interest in implementing ICAO principles of real consultations between AIG, as 
a monopoly supplier, and the airlines.   
 
IATA highly regards your Excellency as a keen supporter of our industry which is 
currently passing through a very difficult time. 
 
Sincerely, 
 
 
 
Giovanni Bisignani 
Director General and CEO 
IATA – International Air Transport Association 
Montreal – Geneva
