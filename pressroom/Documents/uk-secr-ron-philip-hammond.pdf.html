Eruption of the Grimsvötn volcano in Iceland: letter from the 
Director General & CEO of IATA to The Right Hon Philip 
Hammond, Secretary of State for Transport, United Kingdom 

 
 
 

Geneva, 23 May 2011 

 
 
Dear Secretary Hammond, 

 

Further to the eruption of the Grimsvotn volcano in Iceland, I am writing to seek your 
urgent personal intervention please to ensure the earliest availability of the CAA monitoring 
aircraft.  

 

As you know, a major lesson from the volcano incident last year was the importance of 
early, quick and accurate data regarding the location and nature of the volcanic ash plume. 
Whilst ground-based monitoring systems are now in place, I am very concerned to learn 
that the CAA aircraft is unavailable. It is astonishing and unacceptable that Her Majesty’s 
Government cashes £3.5 billion each year in Air Passenger Duty but is incapable of using 
a small portion of that revenue to purchase another Cessna to use as a back-up aircraft. I 
ask please that you ensure that all possible efforts are made to get the existing aircraft 
operational in the shortest possible time.  

 

The IATA team is working closely with your officials, NATS, the European Aviation Crisis 
Coordination Cell and other organisations to ensure the most appropriate response to the 
volcano cloud. There have, most certainly, been improvements since the volcanic eruption 
in April 2011, and I am encouraged by EASA’s issuance of guidance material for States.   
 
Nevertheless, I remain very concerned at this point about the lack of formal agreement at 
the political level on how individual States will handle their regulatory responsibility in the 
declaration of airspace. In particular, non-European registered airlines may encounter 
additional difficulty obtaining access to European airspace as their Regulators will be less 
familiar with the situation. As you know, we need a clear, consistent and appropriate 
response, which is workable for all airlines globally, not a repeat of the fragmented mess 
that happened last year.  

 

Please do not hesitate to contact me if you require further assistance from IATA and the 
airline community.  

 

Sincerely,

 

 
Giovanni Bisignani 
Director General and CEO 
IATA – International Air Transport Association 
Montreal – Geneva
