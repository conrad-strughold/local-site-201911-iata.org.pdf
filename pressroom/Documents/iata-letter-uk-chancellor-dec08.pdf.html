UK Air Passenger Duty:  letter from the Director General of IATA to the UK 
Chancellor of the Exchequer, Rt Hon Alistair Darling MP 

 
 
 

15 December 2008 

 
 
Dear Chancellor, 
 
 
On behalf of the 233 Members of IATA, I would like to express our appreciation that you 
listened to our representations and decided not to replace the Air Passenger Duty (APD) 
with the proposed Aviation Duty. Unfortunately, we cannot regard the announced reform 
of the APD as anything other than bad for passengers, bad for industry and bad for the 
competitiveness of the UK economy. 
 
We maintain that the reformed APD: 

•

  Will not have any effect on aviation's environmental impact and unfairly penalises 

the ailing aviation industry 

•

 Still ignores the fact that the industry already fully compensates for its 

environmental costs 

•

  May constitute an attempt to exert extra-territorial jurisdiction by applying a tax  

– allegedly to address the environmental impact of flights – outside of UK 
airspace 

•

  Will result in double taxation under the EU's Emissions Trading Scheme 

 
Therefore, it must be revoked. 
 
The APD today raises £2 billion annually to the UK Government, supposedly for 
environmental purposes but none of the revenue is earmarked for any such projects. 
According to industry estimates, the 2008 PBR change will raise some additional £460 
million in 2009-10, rising to £720 million by 2011. By 2012, when aviation emissions are to 
be included in the EU ETS, revenues collected for the APD will approach £3 billion per 
year. These figures are alarming and cannot be tolerated by our industry. 
 
The timing could not be worse. IATA recently announced its forecast for industry losses of 
US$ 5 billion this year and US$2.5 billion in 2009. All regions, except the US, are 
expected to report larger losses in 2009 than in 2008. Losses for European carriers will 
increase ten-fold to US$1 billion. Europe's main economies are already in recession. The 
reformed APD will hit UK network carriers badly with severe consequences for the UK 
global economy in the coming years. We fail to understand the logic behind the UK 
Government making tax cuts to help the UK economy recover, while at the same time 
punishing a struggling but very important industry with massive tax rises.

In addition, there are some serious shortfalls in the revised APD scheme and we urge the 
Government to address the following without further delay: 

•

  The rates themselves are disproportionate to actual fare levels 

•

  The four distance bands create discrimination against various destinations and 

cause market distortions 

•

  The measurement system behind the bands has incorrectly placed many 

countries in a higher band 

 
In short, the reform of the Air Passenger Duty is unwarranted and unjustified. The effects 
of its implementation have clearly not been considered properly, through the due 
consultation processes. Therefore, I urge you in the strongest possible terms to withdraw 
the reform proposals immediately. 
 
Sincerely, 
 
 
 
Giovanni Bisignani 
Director General and CEO 
IATA – International Air Transport Association 
Montreal – Geneva
