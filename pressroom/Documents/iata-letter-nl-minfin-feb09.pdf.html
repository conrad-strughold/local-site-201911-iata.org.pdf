Dutch Air Passenger Tax:  letter from the Director General of IATA to the 
Minister of Finance of The Netherlands, Mr W..J. Bos  

 
 

24 February 2009 

 
 
Dear M

iniste

r Bos, 

 
On behalf of IATA and its 230 Member airlines, representing 93% of scheduled 
international air traffic, I am writing to seek urgent action by the Dutch Government to 
remove the Air Passenger Tax that was introduced in July 2008. 
 
Only seven months after its introduction, the Air Passenger Tax has already had a 
serious impact on traffic figures. There has been a decline of almost a million departing 
passengers from Schiphol airport as compared to 2007/2008. 2009 is predicted to be 
even more difficult - the airport predicts a sharp drop in passenger traffic, together with 
an expected decline in air transport movements of up to 10%.  
 
The consequence, as you will agree, is a clear deterioration in the competitiveness of the 
airlines at Schiphol in comparison to other main EU hub airports or even neighbouring 
airports such as Brussels Zaventem or even Düsseldorf and Weeze in Germany.  The 
vital role for the Dutch economy of Schiphol International Airport remaining a main EU 
hub is endangered.  The Dutch Government must take action now to maintain the 
network of connections, which is crucial infrastructure for its open economy.  
 
The Air Passenger Tax has proven to be both disastrous and ineffective - this shift of 
passengers from Schiphol to other airports does nothing to help fight climate change. 
Furthermore, the Air Passenger Tax represents an unacceptable cost burden on the 
aviation industry and an unfair penalty to aviation jobs in the Netherlands at the very time 
that other industries around the world are benefiting from economic crisis recovery 
measures. 
 
IATA welcomes your Government’s announcement to take action to restore the 
international competitive position of Schiphol.  As a significant first step, I urge you to 
withdraw the Air Passenger Tax at the earliest possible moment.  In the current situation, 
there is no time to waste. 
 
Sincerely, 
 
 
Giovanni Bisignani 
Director General and CEO 
IATA – International Air Transport Association 
Montreal – Geneva
