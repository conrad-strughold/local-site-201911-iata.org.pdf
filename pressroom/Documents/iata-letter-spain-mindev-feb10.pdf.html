Efficiency of Spanish ANS:  letter from the Director General of IATA to the 
Minister of Public Works and Transport of Spain, Mr José Blanco López  

 
 
 

Geneva, 10 February 2010 

 
 
Dear Minister, 
 
 
I am writing to offer our full support for the robust measures you are taking to address and 
resolve the efficiency problems with Spanish air navigation services.  
 
IATA has pointed out consistently that the combination of high costs and low productivity of 
AENA air traffic controllers is unacceptable and unaffordable to airlines, in addition to 
being highly damaging to the economic development of Spain. We have made it clear to 
AENA that its airline customers expect cost containment and significant reductions in the 
present charges. The IATA press release of 27 January applauded your commitment to 
address these issues through a progressive strategy for reform of AENA.  
 
We therefore very much welcome the clear and firm actions you have taken in this regard.  
We note that the Council of Ministers’ approval of the Decree of 5 February will enable the 
necessary reform of AENA to provide the required safety, capacity and cost-efficiency for 
Spanish air navigation services. This will greatly facilitate the economic benefits that 
aviation provides to the Spanish tourism and business sectors and to the economy as a 
whole.  
 
I am pleased to note the commitment in the Decree to reduce the Spanish en route 
charges to the average of the five major European Air Navigation Service Providers by 
2013. Based on the existing levels of charges, we estimate this to be a reduction of around 
15% by 2013. This is a very welcome step given the current economic situation and the 
financial crisis in the airline industry. Accordingly, I urge you to ensure the earliest possible 
implementation of the measures contained in the Decree. 
 
If we can be of any further assistance in helping to achieve these objectives please do not 
hesitate to contact me. 
 
Sincerely, 
 
 
 
Giovanni Bisignani 
Director General and CEO 
IATA – International Air Transport Association 
Montreal – Geneva
