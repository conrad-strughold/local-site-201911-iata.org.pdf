Aviation’s environment commitment to the Copenhagen Summit:  letter from the 
Director General of IATA to the Secretary General of the United Nations, 
Mr Ban Ki-moon  

 
 

14 October 2009 

 
 
Dear Mr Secretary General, 
 
It was a great honor and pleasure to meet you and discuss the leadership role the 
international aviation industry is playing in addressing the challenge of climate change.  
I very much appreciate your recognition of our successful efforts to date and your 
endorsement of our aggressive targets moving forward. I also welcome your offer to help 
us convey this positive message to the COP-15 meeting in Copenhagen. 
 
As we discussed, aviation serves as a role model for industries seeking to reduce their 
carbon footprint.  The IATA four-pillar strategy has already resulted in a savings of 
70 

million tons of CO

2

. Our commitments to carbon-neutral growth from 2020, 

1.5% average annual improvement in fuel efficiency until that point and a 50% reduction in 
net emissions by 2050 compared to 2005 levels are more ambitious than what has been 
proposed by the ICAO member states.  The broad industry, including manufacturers, 
airports and air navigation service providers as well as airlines, under the leadership of 
IATA, is united behind these targets and we will meet them. 
 
The 7-9 October ICAO High Level Meeting on International Aviation and Climate Change 
was a preview of the difficult challenges that will be presented at Copenhagen, particularly 
by those countries committed to the principle of Common but Differentiated 
Responsibilities (CBDR).  I am convinced that the positive story of international aviation, 
jointly working with the UN, can serve as an inspiration for other industries and member 
states that are hesitant to take the difficult steps to address this major global challenge. 
 
As you noted, it is important that this positive message be understood and appreciated by 
the delegates to the COP-15 meeting in Copenhagen.  To that end, as you have 
suggested, the international aviation industry, represented by IATA, will present jointly with 
ICAO these strong industry commitments to the COP-15 meeting.  I would ask for your 
strong support for this proposal as we approach the December meeting. 
 
Thank you again for your very warm reception, your generosity with your time and kind 
words of encouragement. 
 
Sincerely, 
 
 
 
Giovanni Bisignani 
Director General and CEO 
IATA – International Air Transport Association 
Montreal – Geneva
