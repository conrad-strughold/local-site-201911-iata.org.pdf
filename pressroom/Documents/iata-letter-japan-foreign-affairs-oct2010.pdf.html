Japan Solidarity Tax:  letter from the Director General & CEO 
of IATA to the Minister of Foreign Affairs of Japan, Mr Seiji 
Maehara 

 
 

Geneva, 5 October 2010 

 
 
Dear Minister Maehara, 
 
 
I am writing to express my concern following recent reports on ongoing discussions for a levy 
on airline tickets to generate funds for world poverty reduction efforts. 
 
The International Air Transport Association (IATA) and its members, together with the 
International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO), are firmly opposed to any taxes on the sale 
and use of international air transport. Any additional charge on aviation should follow the 
ICAO principle on charges, 

i.e.

 that a charge should have as its purpose cost recovery for civil 

aviation services (ref: ICAO Doc 8632). As an ICAO contracting state and as a recently re-
elected and long-term member in good standing of the ICAO Council, Japan should not ignore 
this fundamental principle. 
 
While IATA applauds the honorable goal of funding economic and social development, this 
proposed levy ignores the substantial contribution that air transport already makes to 
economic development and social cohesion. Air transport connects people and businesses to 
each other, as well as the global economy. It contributes greatly to the Japanese economy 
through tourism and other sectors. In 2010, the air transport industry and its dependent 
sectors are expected to generate around 6 million jobs in Japan. Export earnings from 
international visitors and tourism goods are expected to generate USD12.1 billion in 2010 
while travel and tourism investment is estimated at USD59.6 billion (ref: World Travel & 
Tourism Council).  
 
Current visitors arriving to Japan are less than 10 million per year though your Government 
has a goal of 20 million visitors by 2016 and an ultimate goal of 30 million per year. In 
addition, both airlines and the Government have been working hard on increasing the number 
of Japanese travelling overseas to 20 million a year but this would be put at serious risk with 
the introduction of a new levy on air tickets. Passengers are highly price-sensitive and a levy 
on aviation would discourage overall passenger traffic. It would also add a considerable 
burden to your whole export-oriented economy that is highly dependent on being 
internationally connected in an affordable manner. Japanese aviation, tourism and the overall 
economy would suffer.

I firmly believe that these are among the many compelling reasons for not pursuing any further 
the discussion of the air ticket levy. Any subsequent introduction of such a levy by the 
Japanese government will conflict with national interests and will not have the support of the 
Japanese aviation industry, including the airlines that IATA represents. 
 
I look forward to your urgent intervention on the issue. 
 
 
Sincerely, 
 
 
 
Giovanni Bisignani 
Director General and CEO 
IATA – International Air Transport Association 
Montreal – Geneva
