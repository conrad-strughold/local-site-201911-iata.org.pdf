JUL

Established in 1945, the International Air Transport 
Association (IATA) is an international trade association 
for the world’s airlines, with headquarters in Quebec, 
Canada, and executive offices in Geneva, Switzerland. 
In line with its overarching mission to represent, lead and 
serve the airline industry, IATA spearheads numerous 
activities designed to provide passengers with safe and 
secure transportation.

The designation of airport codes, travel agent 
accreditation, cargo accreditation and myriad member 
services are among IATA’s activities. The association 
currently comprises 275 member airlines from 117 
countries, which account for 83% of total air traffic.

ACADEMIC PARTNER

The aviation industry has grown exponentially over 
the last four decades, generating more than 1,300  
new airlines since the early 1970s. As the single largest 
market in the world, Asia will undoubtedly play a pivotal 
role in the future growth of the sector.  

The INAMP delivers breadth and depth of content by

The programme consists of two 2-week segments

The IATA-Nanyang Advanced Management Programme  
in Aviation and Air Transport (INAMP) provides an 
exceptional learning experience specifically designed for 
accomplished aviation professionals, delivering breadth and 
depth of content by combining critical general management 
tenets with vital industry issues. The full-time programme 
consists of 2-week segments held at IATA’s Training  
Centre in Geneva, Switzerland and on NTU’s vibrant 
Singapore campus.

Participants explore the critical drivers of airport  
business, including airport economics, revenue streams 
and organisational structure. Industry experts share 
best practices and insights on the core components of 
successful airport performance.

Leading industry experts share their insights and know-how 
on the key elements of successful airport management. 
Participants expand their understanding of critical industry 
challenges and learn strategies to reconcile growing 
demand with environmental concerns.

In this dynamic course, participants deepen their  
awareness of innovation, global leadership and cross-
cultural management, as well as boost their strategic 
leadership potential.

approach to cost management.
