THE ECONOMIC BENEFITS OF REDUCING FUEL 
TAXES IN THE DOMINICAN REPUBLIC

 

KEY CONCLUSIONS 

Ê

 

Fuel taxes add US$76 million to the cost of fuel uplifted in the Dominican Republic; 

Ê

 

This adds 16.4% to the cost of fuel uplifted in the Dominican Republic, damaging the competitiveness of the 

travel and tourism sector in the Dominican Republic; 

Ê

 

Travel and tourism is already a key sector for the economy, supporting an estimated 17.7% of GDP and 

679,000 jobs in 2011; 

Ê

 

A 50% reduction in the fuel taxes – at a gross cost to the government of US$38 million - would reduce travel 

costs by 1.6%, boost tourist arrivals by almost 90,000, add US$186 million to GDP and support a further 13,000 
jobs in the economy.  The benefit-cost ratio for the economy is significantly higher than one; 

Ê

 

Abolition of these taxes – at a gross cost of US$76 million – would reduce travel costs 3.1%, boost tourist 

arrivals 174,000, add US$355 million to GDP and support 25,000 new jobs.  Economic benefits would be larger;

The majority of passengers arriving by air are travelling for leisure not business.  We use estimates by 

Professor Gillen in his well known study of travel demand price elasticities to estimate the sensitivity of leisure 
passenger arrivals to falls in travel costs.  We find that a 50% reduction in fuel taxes could boost arrivals by 
2.2% or 90,000.  Abolition could improve passenger arrivals 4.2% or 174,000.
