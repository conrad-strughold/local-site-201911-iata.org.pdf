Operation of Aircraft 

Cargo Compartment Safety

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Background

Ø

ICAO Air Navigation Commission raised concerns that the 

risks posed by the transport of cargo by air may not be 

sufficiently mitigated. In particular that lithium batteries may 

overwhelm aircraft systems in the event of a fire.

Ø

Cargo Safety Sub-Group (CSSG) established in Feb 2017 

under the ICAO Flight Operations Panel:

Ø

develop requirements in Annex 6 for operators to conduct safety risk 

assessments on the carriage of cargo;

Ø

develop associated guidance material on conduct of safety risk 

assessment and identifying appropriate mitigations. 

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Composition

Ø

Representatives from:

Ø

Dangerous Goods Panel – Canada, Germany, Italy, Spain, UK, US,

Ø

and Boeing)

Ø

Airworthiness Panel have a separate job card from the ANC 

to address the design capabilities of the aircraft.

Objective

Ø

Annex 6 – Requirements for operators to conduct safety risk 

assessments on carriage of cargo, including the carriage of 

dangerous goods;

Ø

New ICAO Document – Guidance material on conducting 

safety risk assessment, including the carriage of dangerous 

goods.

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Considerations

Ø

Cargo compartment fire suppression systems are certified to 

suppress fires that are likely to occur:

Ø

typically Class A  fires – common combustible materials, e.g. paper, 

wood, clothing.

Ø

Certification and limitations of aircraft cargo compartment 

fire suppression systems:

Ø

what information is provided to the operators by the aircraft 

manufacturers on the capabilities of the aircraft systems?

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Considerations (cont.)

Ø

Expectation by Flight Operations Panel members that the 

operator understands what cargo (and baggage and mail) is 

being offered for transport and the associated risks that may 

be introduced.

Ø

this position was supported by aircraft manufacturers and IFALPA

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Outcome

Ø

Proposed Chapter 15 – Cargo Compartment Safety

Ø

transport of “items” in the cargo compartment, the State of the 

Operator shall ensure that the operator establishes policy and 

procedures, which includes a specific safety risk assessment.

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Outcome (cont.)

Ø

the safety risk assessment must consider: 

§

specific hazard of items being carried;

§

capabilities of the operator;

§

operational considerations;

§

capabilities of the aeroplane and its systems;

§

containment characteristics of ULDs;

§

packing and packaging;

§

safety of the supply chain; and

§

quantity and distribution of dangerous goods.

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Outcome (cont.)

Ø

Fire protection, elements of cargo compartment fire 

protection system and summary of demonstrated cargo 

compartment fire protection certification standards shall be 

provided in aircraft documentation.

Ø

this recognizes that the operators have not been provided with 

sufficient information on the fire suppression capabilities of the 

aircraft by the manufacturers.

Ø

The operator shall establish policies and procedures that address items 

to be carried in the cargo compartment. Ensure to a reasonable 

certainty that a fire can be detected and suppressed, until the aircraft 

makes a safe landing

. 

./events/wcs/wcspptpresentationsandvideos/dangerous_goods_track-html.html

Where are we?

Ø

Proposed amendment to Annex 6 to include Chapter 15 sent 

out to ICAO Member States in a State letter, closing date for 

comments 24 November 2018.

Ø

Comments being considered by Air Navigation Commission  

5 March 2019.

Ø

Chapter 15 becomes effective November 2020.
