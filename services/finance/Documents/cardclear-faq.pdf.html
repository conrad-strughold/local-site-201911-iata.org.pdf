Q. Why do airlines use CardClear?

 There are a number of reasons.

à

To centralise all your different channels credit card 
sales through a single point

à

To enable the airline to set-up Global agreements 
with multi-currency acquirers so that they receive 
centralised settlement and payment for ALL their 
credit card sales. This means that the airline does not 
need to maintain local credit card acquiring 
agreements with banks in each country. This reduces 
overheads in managing these funds locally

à

Centralisation allows the airline to access, report and 
forecast their funds more easily, facilitating treasury 
to manage their funds more easily

à

Funds may be settled in one or more of the major 
currencies instead of the local currency through the 
LCCB arrangement

à

Option of using more than one multi-currency 
acquirer

à

The airline has access to comprehensive, fully 
reconciled reporting via a secure website with user 
access controlled by the airline

à

The web-tool provides the airline with the facility for 
on-line transaction query handling from anywhere in 
the world

à

Option to take daily electronic reconciliation files 
which may be used by the airline to complete the 
accounting process in their Revenue Accounting 
systems to match the Acquirers Electronic Payment 
Advice, CardClear files and the periodic BSP HOTs

à

CardClear provides a dedicated analyst for a single 
point of enquiry and resolution for all the airline 
queries

CardClear has contracts with over 140 airlines and is 
continually adding new airlines to the business.

Q . How does the Airline receive payment on a ticket 
sale?

Yes. CardClear accepts files from airlines and processes 
the transactions in the same way that it handles BSP 
transactions.

This requires a secure technical link between Accelya 
and the airline for file delivery and use of a file format 
that is compatible with both Accelya and the airlines 
systems. For further information, please contact us.

CardClear processing is flexible and the platform has 
functionality to enable variations to suit the airline and 
acquirer needs on processing and reporting. The only 
restrictions tend to be those imposed by the cards 
schemes and restrictions on export of credit card data 
in some parts of the world. Some South American 
countries, India and Bangladesh fall into this category.

Airlines receive payments for tickets direct from their 
acquirer and the card companies based on agreed fees 
and payment terms at the contract stage. The payment 
cycle varies depending on the airline's negotiated 
arrangements with their acquirers. The airline will 
receive regular Payment Advice reports from the 
acquirers. The funds from the settled transactions are 
deposited to the bank accounts that have been specified 
by the airline. Payments are made in the currency 
agreed by the airline with their acquirer. This may be 
local currency in some cases, but most airlines will 
prefer to have deposits of USD, GBP and EUR as these 

are the currencies primarily used for international 
payments for aircraft, aviation fuel, insurance and other 
major overheads and investment.

Accelya's CardClear Service is fully PCI-DSS (Payment 
Card Industry-Data Security Standard) certified since 
January, 2008. Accelya works according to the 
requirements of the PCI program to maintain on-going 
integrity and security of cardholder data on behalf of all 
its customers.

The CardClear team monitors that all expected files are 
accounted for each day and the CardClear system will 
alert if any data received has not been output for 
settlement. The CardClear team also generates regular 
reports to ensure that active Merchant set-ups are 
receiving airline credit card data as expected.

CardClear creates outputs to the Card Companies and 
Acquiring banks MON through FRI to match the 
acquirers’ processing schedule, even on national 
holidays in any country. CardClear has the capability to 
output multiple times per day, every day if necessary.

CardClear currently has links to over 30 card companies 
and acquirers. If an airline customer requested that we 
start a relationship with a different acquirer, it would 
be reviewed by the business. New acquirer connections 
will be developed under request after quotation 
acceptance.

Q . Is the airline charged VAT (Value Added Tax) on 
the CardClear Invoice?

Q . What is the CardClear pricing structure?

EU law requires EU based companies to provide a VAT 
number. There are three possibilities:

1.  If the airline is EU based and making payment from 
within the EU, then a VAT number is required. If the VAT 
number is used on the invoice then the airline does not 
have to pay VAT.

2.  If the airline is EU based and is making payment from 
the airline ES office, then VAT will be applied to the 
invoice.

3.  If the airline is NOT an EU based airline and is 
making payment from outside of the ES, VAT does not 
apply.

CardClear pricing is tier based. The higher the 

transaction volumes, the lower the price per 
transaction.
